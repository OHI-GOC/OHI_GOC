Expert Working Group 3.1

Thursday, July 16, 2026 | 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM (PST)

Welcome to EWG 3.1! During this meeting, we are presenting and discussing final results for the Gulf of California Ocean Health Index for the OHI goals Tourism & Recreation, Food Provisioning, and Livelihoods & Economies. Our objectives are to clearly explain the goal model, flag regional scores that do not make sense, and review any major concerns you might have. Due to time constraints, we will try not to discuss the finer details or data layers associated with each model extensively.

Overview of this summer’s EWG meetings

EWG 3.1 (July 16, 2026)

Goals under review: Tourism & Recreation, Food Provisioning, Livelihoods & Economies

Tourism & Recreation (TR) captures the quantity, quality, and sustainability of experiences that people have when visiting coastal and marine areas. It focuses on the personal and cultural value of coastal recreation, not on revenue or livelihoods generated by tourism (those are covered under Livelihoods & Economies). The TR model combines three components: tourist arrivals (quantity), safety and availability of activities (quality), and certifications, job quality, and tourism management investment (sustainability).

Food Provisioning (FP) measures the amount of seafood that is sustainably produced by wild catch fisheries and mariculture operations in the Gulf of California.

Livelihoods & Economies (LE) measures jobs and revenue from sustainable marine related industries. It has two subgoals: Livelihoods (quantity and quality of marine related jobs) and Economies (revenue from marine related sectors). The model uses INEGI Censos Económicos data to track employment, job quality, and sector revenue across the nine OHI regions.

EWG 3.2 (August 13, 2026)

Goals under review (subject to change): Habitat Services, Access Opportunities, Sense of Place

EWG 3.3 (September 10, 2026)

Goals under review (subject to change): Biodiversity, Clean Waters, Natural Products, Safety & Security*

  • Safety & Security is technically not a goal but will act as a pressure on all goals, reflecting how safety concerns affect people’s ability to benefit from ocean resources.
