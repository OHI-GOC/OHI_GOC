This goal captures the sense that marine resources are not limited by safety concerns.

This information is meant to help guide the process of developing the Gulf of California OHI!

This goal is not currently included in the global OHI, but the Expert Working Group felt that safety and security has a large impact on the Gulf of California system’s ability to sustainably provide people with the benefits and services they both need and desire from marine systems.

Key Questions

Could organized crime be considered a pressure for the safety and security goal?

Or does the safety and security goal seem to be more of a pressure on many other goals, and should therefore not be a goal itself?

Are there other ways to highlight the importance of this concept without having it as a separate goal?

Considerations

  • Could separate organized crime from other pressures in the framework
  • Explore “Hugs not Drugs” approach (referencing social programs as alternatives)
  • Account for perceived differences among the five states (e.g., Sinaloa vs. Sonora)

Social Justice and Green Resilience

  • Could think about social justice as a resilience metric in the global context, emphasizing equality of opportunities.
  • Could consider green areas as a form of resilience, possibly using a UN data index.
This goal will require sensitivity in language and verbage used.
